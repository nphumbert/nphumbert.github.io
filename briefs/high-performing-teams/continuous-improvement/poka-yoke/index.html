Poka-Yoke

  • Nadia Zablah Humbert-Labeaumaz
  • July 11, 2026
  • 1 min read

Get the PDF

Enter your email to access this document.

Core Idea

Even well-designed processes remain vulnerable to human error, regardless of an individual’s experience or competence.

Poka-Yoke, a Japanese term meaning “mistake-proofing,” aims to prevent errors by designing systems that either make mistakes impossible or immediately detect them before they result in defects.

Ultimately, the essence of Poka-Yoke is to improve quality by making the correct action the easiest, if not the only, possible action.

Application

Review the standardized process and identify activities where errors are most likely to occur or where their consequences would be most significant.

Where possible, redesign these activities to prevent or detect mistakes immediately. Typical examples include:

  • Connectors that fit in only one orientation
  • Mandatory fields in digital forms
  • Barcode validation before shipment
  • Safety interlocks on industrial equipment
  • Automated completeness checks
  • Checklists for critical procedures

The most effective Poka-Yoke mechanisms are simple, inexpensive, and integrated directly into the process.

Takeaway

The most reliable way to reduce errors is to design systems that prevent them from occurring in the first place.
